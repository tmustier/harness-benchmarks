Evidence review

The harness (still) matters

Public studies show that the agent harness changes performance, cost, token use and runtime. They do not identify one harness that wins across every model and task.

Evidence captured on 18 July 2026

Executive summary

Current observations

  • Harness choice can materially change results. Claw-SWE-Bench found 12.5- to 27.4-point spreads; Harvey's intervention ranged from 0.9 points worse to 23 points better.
  • No harness wins across all models and tasks. Native CLIs led 4 of 6 current Terminal-Bench pairs, while alternative harnesses led elsewhere.
  • Efficiency differences are often clearer than quality differences. Databricks, GitHub and several smaller studies found large cost, context or token gaps beside modest quality changes.
  • The count is not 22 independent replications. Some studies reuse benchmark families or live leaderboards, and many rely on a single attempt per task.
  • We only count model-fixed comparisons as harness evidence. FrontierSWE and model-only leaderboards remain on the watchlist until they expose a matched harness comparator.
  • Teams should test harnesses on their own work. Compare at least 2 harnesses with the same model, effort and budget. Measure quality, cost and time.

Currently, we know of 22 public harness comparisons and diagnostics

21 hold the model fixed and measure task quality. One isolates prompt overhead.

01Operational and broad evidence8 Jul

Databricks ran 6 matched harness comparisons: Claude Code or Codex cost 1.20 to 2.08 times as much as Pi, with quality within 7 points.

What they did

Databricks replayed recent human-written pull requests from its multi-million-line internal codebase. It compared Claude Code with Pi on Opus 4.8 and Codex with Pi on GPT-5.5 at matched effort settings.

Tasks
Not published
Repeated trials
not clear
Grader
Repository tests reviewed by engineers

What we observe

Pi was cheaper in all 6 published pairs. It also re-fed less context: 236,000 versus 742,000 tokens on Opus 4.8 and 665,000 versus 1.235 million on GPT-5.5.

Limit

The source does not publish task count, run-level data or uncertainty. Per-task dollar costs: three combinations are stated exactly in the text; the rest are derived from the published cost ratios or traced from Figure 1.
